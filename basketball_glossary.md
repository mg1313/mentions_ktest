A

Airball – A shot attempt that misses the rim, backboard, and net entirely.
And-1 – A situation where a player is fouled while successfully making a field goal and is awarded one free throw.
Assist – A pass that directly leads to a made basket.

B

Backcourt – The area of the court between the midcourt line and a team’s own baseline; also refers to a team’s guards.
Baseline – The boundary line under each basket at the ends of the court.
Buzzer Beater – A shot that is released before the game clock expires and counts if it goes in after the buzzer sounds.
Box Out – A rebounding technique where a player positions between an opponent and the basket.

C

Charge – An offensive foul where the ball-handler runs into a defender with established position.
Clean Block – A legal defensive block where the defender avoids significant contact.
Clutch – Performing well under high-pressure situations late in a game.
Corner Three – A three-point shot taken from the corners, where the arc is shortest.

D

Defense – The team without possession attempting to prevent scoring.
Defensive Three-Second Violation – A defensive player remains in the painted area for more than three seconds without actively guarding an opponent (NBA rule).
Double Dribble – An illegal dribble caused by restarting a dribble after stopping or dribbling with both hands.

E

Elbow – The corner of the free-throw line and the lane line on the court.
Excessive Timeout – A team calling more timeouts than allowed.

F

Fast Break – A rapid offensive move following a defensive rebound or turnover.
Field Goal (FG) – Any basket scored from the floor during play (2 or 3 points).
Flagrant Foul – A severe personal foul involving excessive or violent contact.
Free Throw (FT) – An unopposed shot from the free-throw line awarded after certain fouls.

G

Guard – A backcourt player position, typically responsible for ball handling and playmaking.

H

Half Court – The area of the court on either side of the midcourt line.
Hand Check – Using hands to impede a ball-handler’s movement (restricted under current NBA rules).
Hook Shot – A one-handed shot in which the shooter’s body shields the ball from the defender.

I

Inbound – A play where the ball is passed into play from out-of-bounds.
Intentional Foul – A deliberate foul aimed at stopping the clock or gaining advantage.

J

Jump Ball – A method to begin play where the referee tosses the ball between two players.
Jump Shot – A shot taken while the player is in mid-air after jumping.

L

Layup – A close-range shot taken while moving toward the basket.
Lane (Key) – The rectangular area extending from the baseline to the free-throw line.

M

MVP – Most Valuable Player (season award).
Motion Offense – An offensive scheme emphasizing player movement and passing.

N

Net – The mesh hanging from the rim.

O

Offense – The team with possession trying to score.
Overtime (OT) – Additional period(s) played when the score is tied after regulation.
Outlet Pass – A defensive rebound pass to start a fast break.

P

Paint – The rectangular area inside the lane.
Pick (Screen) – A stationary defender positions to impede a defender and free a teammate.
Pivot – The action of keeping one foot stationary while moving the other.
Possession Arrow – A rule used in some leagues to determine alternating possession on held balls (NBA uses jump balls except for start of game).
Power Forward – A frontcourt player typically strong in rebounding and inside scoring.

Q

Quick Release – Shooting the ball immediately upon receiving it.

R

Rebound – Gaining possession after a missed shot.
Referee (Official) – An official who enforces the rulebook.
Restricted Area – An arc underneath the basket where defenders cannot draw charging fouls.
Rip Through – A move where a ball-handler moves the ball from one side to the other to evade a defender.

S

Slam Dunk – A shot where a player thrusts the ball down into the hoop.
Steal – A defensive action where a defender legally takes the ball from an offensive player.
Step Through – A move by a driver who steps around a defender after picking up the dribble.
Substitution – Replacing one player with another.
Screen – Another term for a pick.

T

Technical Foul (T-Foul) – A violation for unsportsmanlike conduct or procedural rules.
Three-Point Field Goal – A basket scored from beyond the three-point line, worth three points.
Traveling – Taking too many steps without dribbling (violation).
Timeout – A stoppage of play called by a team or official.

U

Under Basket – Area directly beneath the rim.

V

Violation – A rules infraction (e.g., traveling, double dribble).
Visual Screen – A screen where the defender does not see the pick set.

W

Wing – Area of the court on the perimeter near the three-point line.
Wild Shot – An uncontrolled or off-balance shot attempt.
